# One-shot portfolio revival pass (not an engine)

Idea seed captured 2026-07-19 for a future exploration session. The read below is tentative — confirm intent with Jesse before designing.

**Tentative read:** There is a real inventory of started-and-parked work — the Shift-Delta job-cost tracker (spec clean and back-tested, unbuilt), the `leverage` repo's five queued build packets and its two layers that never fired, Knowledge Loop OS sessions C/D/F, three vault stashes from the Fable-branch experiments, and the stalled thesis experiment. A single bounded pass over memory, `INDEX.md`, and the repo that produces a ranked revive/complete/kill list would surface all of it in about an hour. The explicit finding from the 2026-07-19 leverage investigation was that this should stay a one-time pass and never become a standing "portfolio recovery engine" — the inventory is short enough to read directly, and an engine to rediscover a known list is exactly the kind of meta-system that dies unused.

**To explore:** Whether the list is better produced on demand than captured now, since anything written down today is stale the moment a packet gets built or killed. What the kill criterion should be — the `leverage` promotion rules already say a task with no runs in 90 days is a prune candidate rather than a maintenance obligation, and that rule may be all that's needed. Whether the three vault stashes should simply be reviewed and dropped rather than tracked. Whether the output belongs in the decision queue as individual rows (each parked item is arguably one decision) instead of as a standalone note that itself becomes another parked artifact.
