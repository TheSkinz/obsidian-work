# Durable knowledge from USA26038: the three-document model + work-up billing math

Captured 2026-07-11 for routing into `04-knowledge/` (near `01-context/estimating-approach` and the estimating rollup). Most of the USA26038 job-sheet session was spent re-deriving these distinctions from scratch — writing them down once prevents re-litigating them every job.

## The three USADeBusk field-document types

- **Job sheet** — static, created at bid-win from the quoted work-up. Crew-facing, printable. Holds the quoted resource plan and how to key service receipts. Never carries actuals, status, or timeline.
- **Heater card** — persistent. The heater's fixed physical facts plus accumulated actuals (Task Durations, Field Notes) across all jobs on that unit.
- **Job report** — post-job. Timeline and actuals for one job (what really happened, who was really there, real hours). This is where the quoted-vs-actual gaps live.

Corollary confirmed this session: **equipment is not pre-assigned to a heater** on the job sheet (which rig works which heater is a field decision); supervisors are fixed to a rig, operators are a shared pool by shift.

## Work-up billing math (per-heater Qty / Item / Amt / Unit table)

- Shift basis: day-shifts × 12h (last may be partial) + night-shifts × 12h = heater total pumper hours.
- **Labor Amt = combined man-hours** = Qty × per-person shift hours (e.g. 2 Day Operators over 44h/person = 88).
- Equipment (pumper, support unit, filter unit, crew truck) billed at the heater's pumper total hours.
- Per Diem billed per day; headcount **excludes the billable Project Manager** (matches the night side's Sup + Ops pattern).
- DEF billed per shift; use the quoted shift count as given (not derived from total shift count).
- A billable Project Manager line appears per-heater only when the quote carries one (USA26038: on H-20, not H-19).

Note the quote's resource plan can differ from the actual mobilized crew (USA26038: quoted 12, sent 10) — the job sheet reflects the quote; the actuals reconcile on the job report.
