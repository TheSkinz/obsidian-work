## Field Notes

### USA26025 — March/April 2026

**Fouling character:** Heavy pitch and resid-based fouling across all 8
passes, both convection and radiant. Each pass diesel-flushed 2-3 hrs before
pigging to mobilize pitch; diesel collected in frac tank. Fouling concentrated
in the 3" Sch 80 tubes, specifically the last 6 tubes before the 3"→4"
reducer — consistent across all passes. Isolated coke rings in a small number
of passes in that section. The 3" tubes required the most sustained
mechanical effort of any tube size on the heater.

**Execution:** TriMax 2 ran Triple Mode throughout (3 passes simultaneously).
Pigging began Apr 4 on Passes 2A/3A/8A after the 9-day plant-caused standby;
Pass 1A joined shortly after. Progression through the 3.068" convection tubes
was slow with heavy effluent tracking. 3.25" pigs were extremely difficult to
run and advanced only where tube condition permitted (see Max Pig OD note).
Pass 3A required on-site removal/replacement of a non-conforming 3" Sch 160
bend that was obstructing pigging. Pass 8A hit a coke obstruction requiring
hydroblasting — USADeBusk disconnected temporarily and pigged Pass 6A in the
interim to maintain progress, then returned to Pass 8A once hydroblasting/
diesel circulation finished. Pass 8A remained the critical path throughout.
All 8" sections were hydroblasted before mechanical pigging on every pass.

**Flow test results (pre/post PSI):** Largest reduction Pass 8A (−190 PSI),
then Pass 3A (−185 PSI). Full pass-by-pass flow test table available in
source PDF if needed for a future proposal's Verification of Pass Cleanliness
section.

**Combined pig utilization (H-102A + H-102B, 434 total):** wide size range
2"-8", concentrated at 3" (97), 3.125" (52), 5" (49), 4" (34) — consistent
with the 3" tube fouling severity noted above.

**Quote vs. Actual:** Quoted $553,830.59 (360 project hrs) vs. actual billed
$879,040.31 — $325,209.72 over (59% overrun), driven by the plant-caused
standby and heavier-than-expected fouling. Per Jesse: this was an emergency
mobilization with a chaotic schedule, which explains both the standby and the
overrun.
