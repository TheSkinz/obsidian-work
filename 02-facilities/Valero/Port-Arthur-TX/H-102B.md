## Field Notes

### USA26025 — March/April 2026

**Fouling character:** Same heavy pitch/resid fouling as H-102A across all 8
passes, diesel-flushed 2-3 hrs before pigging. Fouling again concentrated in
the 3" Sch 80 tubes before the 3"→4" reducer. Pass 2B was the most fouled
pass on the heater — heavy tracking throughout, multiple stuck-pig events
across the 4" and 5" sections, confirmed by flow test as the highest pressure
drop on H-102B (−190 PSI).

**Execution:** TriMax 5 ran H-102B from the start through Apr 11, when TriMax
6 took over (rigged into Passes 4B/7B/8B within 2 hrs of arrival, no
interruption to pigging). Both units ran Triple Mode. Pass 2B was the primary
focus for the first two weeks. Pass 7B ran behind schedule from intermittent
delays but completed without issue. **Pass 6B was the final bottleneck of the
entire project:** smart pig inspection found a 0.25" thick circumferential
coke ring at the inlet end of the second 5" tube (last 5" tube before the
step-up to the 8" section), requiring ~24 hrs of dedicated pigging with 4.5"
pigs to clear before final sign-off — broken the night of Apr 16-17 and
confirmed clear by Quest Integrity before rig-out. Pass 6B recorded the
second-highest pressure drop on the heater (−188 PSI).

**Flow test results (pre/post PSI):** Largest reductions Pass 2B (−190 PSI),
Pass 6B (−188 PSI). Full pass-by-pass flow test table available in source PDF
if needed for a future proposal's Verification of Pass Cleanliness section.

**Combined pig utilization (H-102A + H-102B, 434 total):** wide size range
2"-8", concentrated at 3" (97), 3.125" (52), 5" (49), 4" (34) — consistent
with the 3" tube fouling severity and the Pass 6B 5" coke ring noted above.

**Quote vs. Actual:** Quoted $553,830.59 (360 project hrs) vs. actual billed
$879,040.31 — $325,209.72 over (59% overrun), driven by the plant-caused
standby and heavier-than-expected fouling, including the Pass 6B bottleneck.
Per Jesse: this was an emergency mobilization with a chaotic schedule, which
explains both the standby and the overrun.
