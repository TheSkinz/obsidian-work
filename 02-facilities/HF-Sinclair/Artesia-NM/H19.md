## Field Notes

### USA25051 — August 2025

**Pigs Ran:** (not recorded)

**Crew / Ticket Reference:** See H-19 timeline tickets 8952–8967 for shift-by-shift detail (dayshift crew: James Lee, Randy Smith, Damion Charles; nightshift crew: Rodney Lynch, Raymond Brown, Tyler Garza).

**Obstacles:** Heavy convection fouling near the 4"→3" radiant inlet reducers (tubes 30–40 of both coils). Early 3" radiant pigging was hindered by ID transitions and severe fouling; breakthrough came only after staged convection cleaning raised circuit flow to 50–60 GPM, at which point 3.8" pigs could traverse. Post-decoke dewatering with an 850 CFM compressor failed — the combined pass circuit length and ID transitions caused an air-lock; resolved only after removing the temp 180° loop and installing two 4" launchers directly on the radiant outlet flanges.

**Facility Procedures:** Modified fill/flush procedure — discharge hoses routed from the outlet launcher directly to frac tanks (not the pumper's dirty tank) to minimize crew H₂S exposure. All water filtered and stored in Sinclair-provided frac tanks.

**Recommendation carried forward:** Do not repeat the temp 180° radiant loop between Pass 1 and Pass 2. Keeping the passes independent (Option 1 or Option 2 in the USA25051 report) enables parallel pigging, restores access to the 4" radiant outlet tubes, and removes the dewatering air-lock risk. Confirm with HF Sinclair whether either reconfiguration was implemented before USA26038 scope is finalized.
