## Field Notes

### USA25051 — August 2025

**Pigs Ran:** (not recorded)

**Per-Pass Timing:** Convection pass 1&2 and pass 3&4 each ran 62h (5h rig-in, 51h pig, 6h rig-out); radiant (all 4 passes) ran 94h (6h rig-in, 82h pig, 6h rig-out) — sub-splits of the heater-total Task Durations row above, not separate jobs.

**Crew:** dayshift Danilo Ramirez, Jamel George, Damion Charles; nightshift James McDaniel, Sid Servin, Adrian Garza.

**Obstacles:** Convection coils (4") extremely fouled, deposits ½"–1" concentrated near convection outlets; coke was hard but broke apart quickly once 3.75" pigs were introduced. Radiant coils (2.9"/3.068" ID) heavily fouled, ½" average, concentrated at radiant inlets across all passes; pig travel times ran 12–15 min/pass due to the 4-pass single-circuit length, and dewatering was difficult (though ultimately successful) because of the extended circuit.

**Facility Procedures:** Same modified fill/flush procedure as H19 — discharge routed to frac tanks, not the pumper's dirty tank, to limit H₂S exposure. All water filtered and stored in Sinclair-provided frac tanks.

**Recommendation carried forward:** Reconfigure the radiant section from a single 4-pass circuit into two 2-pass circuits (grade-level launchers on Pass 3 & 4 inlets mirroring Pass 1 & 2; keep temp 180° spools at Pass 1&3 and Pass 2&4 outlet flanges). This halves circuit length/pig travel distance, enables parallel pigging, cuts radiant pig travel from 12–15 min to ~6–8 min, and simplifies dewatering. Confirm with HF Sinclair whether this was implemented before USA26038 scope is finalized.

### USA26038 — July 2026

**As-built config (recommendation validated):** The USA25051 carry-forward was implemented — the radiant ran as **4 coils looped to 2 passes** via temp 180's connecting the Pass 1&3 and Pass 2&4 radiant outlet flanges. This differs from the quote (which still carried the radiant looped to 1 pass). Getting there was staged: **Pass 4 was plugged prior to the decoke**, so its radiant coil was isolated first — launchers/receivers installed at the Pass 4 radiant inlet & outlet flanges and cleaned independently — then, once its restrictions cleared, the heater was set to the 2-pass looped config, which held for the rest of the job. Pumper TriMax 3 / Press 1.

**Actuals:** Rig-in 5 h · pigging 98 h · rig-over 1.5 h · rig-out 13.5 h · stand-by 19 h. Working total 118 h (vs. 80 h quoted). Condition **crash** (emergency). On H-19 completion the H-20 rigging was rigged over to TriMax 5 / Press 2 for the final dewater/rig-out; TriMax 3 / Press 1 demobilized. Full timeline on the [[USA26038-job-report]].

**Deposit localization (per-coil):** Fouling severity **Pass 3 ≫ Pass 4 > Pass 1 ≈ Pass 2**. Pass 4 (plugged) was actually *less* fouled than Pass 3 once opened. Passes 1 & 2 moderate. **Pass 3 radiant was severely restricted — three separate heavy restrictions.** TriMax 3 forced the restriction out with its own pumps; 2" pigs run to establish flow returned badly damaged, and pigs were forced through one-way until enough flow was established to pig bi-directionally.

**Flow tests:** Pass 1&3, Pass 2&4, Pass 3&4 convection-only, and the isolated Pass 4 radiant-only recorded — see job report (some post-decoke columns pending finalization).
