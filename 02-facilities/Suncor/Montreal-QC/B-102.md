# B-102 Crude Furnace — Suncor Montreal, QC

> ⚠ MIXED METALLURGY WITHIN RADIANT COIL — Each radiant leg transitions from 5Cr-½Mo (6"φ main run) to 9Cr-1Mo (8"φ transition) to 347H stainless (8"φ outlet header) per the radiant BOM. Three alloy zones in series, not two — flag for passivation/SOP planning.

> ⚠ CONVECTION AND RADIANT ARE SEPARATE DECOKE CIRCUITS — confirmed 2026-07-07 (Jesse): convection is 4" Sch 40 only (no 6" tubes in convection — the earlier "mixed 4"/6" convection" read was wrong); the 8"×6" reducer sits on the crossover piping between convection and radiant, and that crossover is removed/bypassed for decoke. Convection loops to its own single circuit (16 tubes, 8/pass × 2 passes); radiant loops to its own single circuit (2 legs). This is why there are 5 separate 4"-300# connection points (Mk1-Mk5) — they close off each circuit independently, not one continuous West-to-East path through the whole heater. Max pig OD is governed by the convection 4" run (confirmed 4.026" ID) since it's smaller than radiant's 6" run (confirmed 6.065" ID).

## Field Notes

### RFQ Intake — 2026-07

**Obstacles (resolved):** This card went through four revisions before settling — worth recording so the same rework isn't repeated. (1) P&ID only: convection modeled as 4 independent all-4" coils. (2) Radiant BOM: correctly found the 3-zone radiant metallurgy transition but wrongly assumed convection shared the 6" pipe footage. (3) First elevation-drawing read: convection fixed at 4"-only/16 tubes, radiant read as 44×6" + 12×8" = 56. (4) Final read of a numbered elevation drawing (French labels, per-leg numbering 1-44 with the 6"/8" boundary at label 38): Jesse corrected radiant to 32×6" + 12×8" = 44 total — the 8" count held steady across both drawing reads, but the 6" count dropped from 44 to 32. Final: 16 convection + 44 radiant = 60 tubes heater-total. My BOM-return-bend proxy (~60-62 for the 6" zone alone) was the least reliable source of the three used on this card — treat fitting-count proxies as a rough sanity check only, never as a substitute for a direct tube count off an elevation drawing.

**Facility Procedures:** Decoke connection requires nitrogen purge before removing spool sections 1-5 and 11-15 (per site inspection report notes on drawing 010-600B1202). Site provides its own soda ash/frack tank + filter press + open-atmosphere basin arrangement for this heater's decoke water handling — confirm scope split against USADeBusk's standard filtration equipment before quoting.
