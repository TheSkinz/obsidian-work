> [!warning] Step change in bore — 3.826" convection to 4.813" radiant
> This is the defining feature of the heater and it caused the job's only stuck pig. The card's
> convection/radiant ID mismatch is a **4"→5" reducer** in the flow path. Any pig sized for the radiant
> will not pass the convection, and a pig sized for the convection will under-clean the radiant.

## Field Notes

### USA25025 — 2025 Plant-Wide TA

**Stuck pig — 2025-04-01 night (8787).** Pig stuck in the **4"→5" reducer**. Still stuck at end of
shift. Recovered and coil pigged clean with oversized pigs by end of day shift 2025-04-02 (8788).

**Cleaning result:** pigged until clean at shift change 2025-04-02. No smart pig verification on this
heater per quote scope.

**Estimating note for next TA:** size the pig train to the 3.826" convection bore and plan a separate
radiant strategy, or expect to lose a shift to a reducer hang-up. The 5" pigs that worked the radiant
are the ones at risk at the transition. [[HP-0025]] has the same two-bore feature and was pigged
without incident because it was planned for — that contrast is the lesson.
