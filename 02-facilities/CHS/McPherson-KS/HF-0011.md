### Shift narrative (receipts 8792–8796)

Pigged by section, matching the four-bore coil. Pass 1 & 3 first — polishing the 6", 10" and 8" sections,
running 6.25" TC/HR/Foam, 8.25" TC and 10.25" TC with 12" swabs to dewater. Pass 3 reached smart-pig
readiness ahead of Pass 1, which was still polishing its 6" section into the following shift. Launchers
were then moved to Pass 2 & 4 and the 8" and 10" sections pigged before smart pigging.

> [!important] Smart pig could not get clean data on first attempts
> On the overnight smart-pig run (8793), "air and particulates prevented smart pig from obtaining
> sufficient data." All sections were run with several attempts before data was bought off around shift
> change. A four-bore vacuum coil entrains air at the size transitions — budget repeat smart-pig runs
> here rather than a single pass.

Final rig-out April 5–6: 10" and 6" launchers removed from the furnace connections, press cleaned,
tanks washed, hoses picked up.

> [!warning] Ticket 8796 — 8 hrs rig-out unbilled (INTERNAL)
> The 04-05 night rig-out shift (8796, 8 hrs rig-out + support + crew truck) is signed by the customer
> but has no entry in the billing workbook — the NIGHT (16) tab is empty. Eight hours of documented
> TriMax 1 rig-out went unbilled. The report and the heater-total hours above reflect the **billed**
> figure to stay tied to the auditable record; the field-documented total is 8 hrs higher. See
> [[USA25025-job-record]] commercial open items.

**Estimating note:** 5,004' across four bores with filtration and soda-ash premix took 96 pigging hours
plus 18 smart-pig hours at routine fouling, pigged as two pass-pairs (1 & 3, then 2 & 4). The 51 idle
hours are a scheduling artifact of running two units against nine heaters, not a property of this heater
— exclude them when using this job to estimate. Budget repeat smart-pig runs: the four-bore coil
entrained air and particulates that defeated single-pass inspection. Confirm actual bore distribution
before the next bid: the 64/4/4 tube split is design data only, and design bore proved wrong on two of
the nine heaters here.
