**CONDITIONS OF PASSES**

Light to moderate fouling was observed in the lower radiant sections of all eight coils, with the heaviest deposits concentrated in radiant tubes 29, 30, and 31 near the outlet. Once resid was cleared from each coil, these tubes were specifically targeted with oversized 6.25” pigs for the majority of the decoke. A substantial amount of pitch (cooled, tar-like resid) remained in the coils due to incomplete furnace de-inventory prior to shut-down. To address this, USA DeBusk crews applied Kicksolve circulation in combination with specialized pigs, which assisted in it’s removal and prevented further delays.

**LAUNCHER CONFIGURATION**

* **Passes 2-7:** Launchers located at control valve station and radiant outlet flanges underneath heater.
* **Passes 1 & 8:** Launchers at radiant outlets. Convection inlet spools removed and was replaced with temporary 180° which created a looped single circuit pass.

**RESULTS**

| Duration | 24 hrs avg per coil |

**CLEANING APPROACH**

* **Chemical Treatment:** Kicksolve circulation applied to all coils at initial fill to break up and remove pitch deposits, with additional applications as needed based on return conditions. Soda ash passivation maintained throughout operations with continuous pH monitoring.
* **Inspection:** Smart pig inspection by Quest Integrity/TEAM performed on each pass after completion of cleaning.
* **Passes 2-7:** 7 / 8-minute pig travel time from inlet launcher to outlet launcher.
* **Passes 1 & 8:** Looped at convection inlets. 15 / 16-minute pig travel times from pass 1 radiant outlet launcher to pass 8 radiant outlet launcher.
