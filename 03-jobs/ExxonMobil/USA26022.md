PART 3 — CAUSE DETAIL
01 Heater Preparation Delays — 5/2 and 5/3
Both TriMax units were spotted, staged, and ready for execution when crews arrived on 5/2. The F-802 was not ready to
receive coil fill due to ongoing heater preparation activities and overhead crane lifts in the unit. USADeBusk personnel and
equipment remained on standby until the unit was turned over. Approximately 58 standby hours accumulated across both
units before coil fill could begin.
02 Filter Press Cleaning — Excessive Coke Fines
Abnormally high coke fines content in the effluent caused the filter press to reach capacity twice during active decoking
operations. Each occurrence required a full shutdown of pigging operations to drop the filter cake, wash the filter cloths, and
flush hoses before work could resume. On a standard job the press is dropped once after the decoke is complete. These two
unplanned shutdowns added approximately 40 standby hours across both pumpers
NOTE Materials Variance
Additional pigs were required due to extreme fouling conditions and tube ID changes within the F-802 coils. This is
reflected in the materials variance shown in Section 1.
