## Decoking Summary

### H-102A — Passes 1A through 8A

TriMax Unit 2 | 27 hrs rigging | 232 hrs pigging | 16 hrs smart pig | 176 hrs stand-by

**Fouling character:** Heavy pitch and resid-based fouling across all 8 passes in both convection and radiant sections. Each pass was flushed with diesel for 2–3 hours prior to pigging; diesel collected in a frac tank. Fouling was concentrated in the 3" Schedule 80 tubes, specifically the last 6 tubes before the 3"→4" reducer — consistent across all passes. Isolated coke rings identified in a small number of passes within that section. The 3" tubes required the most sustained mechanical effort of any tube size on the heater.

**Execution:** TriMax 2 operated in Triple Mode throughout, running three passes simultaneously. Pigging commenced April 4 on Passes 2A, 3A, & 8A following the nine-day plant-caused standby; Pass 1A entered operations shortly after and ran concurrently. Progression through the 3.068" convection tubes was slow with heavy effluent tracking, consistent with pitch-heavy fouling. The effective final cleaning diameter in the 3" tube range was 3.125"; 3.25" pigs were extremely difficult to run and advanced only where tube condition permitted. Pass 3A required removal and replacement of a non-conforming 3" Schedule 160 bend obstructing pigging operations — replaced with correct specification on-site and pigging resumed. Pass 8A encountered a coke obstruction requiring hydroblasting; USADeBusk disconnected temporarily from Pass 8A to allow hydroblasting crew access, and pigged Pass 6A productively in the interim. Once hydroblasting and diesel circulation were complete, operations returned to Pass 8A immediately. All 8" sections were hydroblasted prior to mechanical pigging on all passes. Pass 8A was the critical path throughout.

### H-102B — Passes 1B through 8B

TriMax Unit 5 / Unit 6 | 34.5 hrs rigging | 242.5 hrs pigging | 31 hrs smart pig | 179 hrs stand-by

**Fouling character:** Same pitch and resid-heavy fouling character as H-102A across all 8 passes. All passes flushed with diesel 2–3 hours prior to pigging; diesel stored in frac tank. Fouling concentrated in the 3" Schedule 80 tubes, particularly the last 6 tubes before the 3"→4" reducer, with isolated coke rings in a small number of passes. Pass 2B was the most fouled pass on the heater, with heavy tracking throughout and multiple stuck pig events across the 4" and 5" sections — confirmed by flow test as the highest pressure drop reduction on H-102B at −190 PSI. Pass 6B caused the final bottleneck: smart pig inspection revealed a 0.25" thick circumferential coke ring at the inlet end of the second 5" tube (last 5" tube before the step-up to 8"), requiring approximately 24 hours of dedicated pigging with 4.5" pigs to clear before final sign-off. Pass 6B recorded −188 PSI.

**Execution:** TriMax Unit 5 operated on H-102B from the start of pigging through April 11, when it was replaced by TriMax Unit 6. TriMax 6 rigged into Passes 4B, 7B, & 8B within two hours of arrival and pigging resumed without interruption. Both units ran Triple Mode throughout. Pass 2B was the primary focus for the first two weeks; progression through 4" and 5" sections required sustained effort across multiple shifts. Pass 7B ran behind schedule due to intermittent delays but completed without issue. The Pass 6B coke ring was the last obstruction cleared on the entire project — broken on the night of April 16–17 and confirmed clear by Quest Integrity smart pig before rig-out.
