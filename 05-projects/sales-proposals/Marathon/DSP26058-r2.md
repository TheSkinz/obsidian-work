Verification of Pass Cleanliness

USA DeBusk uses a defined, field-proven process to verify decoking pass cleanliness

that combines operator-interface data, visual observations, and physical confirmation.

This verification is performed jointly with the client representative to confirm all

acceptance criteria are met.

1.  Monitor operator-interface data and effluent returns during decoking. Real-

time sensor data is displayed on the operator interface. When coke is

encountered, the display shows characteristic spikes, allowing the technician to

pause the pig and “scrub” specific areas as needed. The technician also monitors

the return effluent for decoking fines (“black”). As the pass cleans up, the

presence of “black” decreases to negligible levels.

2.  Use bi-directional pigging and adjust tooling as required. Because USA

DeBusk performs bi-directional pigging, the pig repeatedly contacts any

remaining coke on the tube wall. The technician evaluates pig condition and

makes adjustments to pig size and tooling as required to achieve complete coke

removal.

3.  Complete a witnessed final gauge foam run for acceptance. When the

interface no longer indicates fouling, the effluent runs clear, and the pig shows no

tracking or discoloration, the shift leader invites the client representative to

witness the final gauge foam run. The foam—slightly oversized to ensure full wall

contact—will mark or deform if any coke remains. The foam is reviewed with the

client, and final cleanliness is accepted once criteria are satisfied.

This stepped verification approach has consistently achieved client approval in the field

and has also proven effective in obtaining smart pig data on the first run.
