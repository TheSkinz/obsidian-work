Verification of Pass Cleanliness

USA DeBusk uses a combination of visual indicators, data review, and physical feedback to
determine the cleanliness of a decoking pass. This process is completed and reviewed side by side
with the representative from the client to ensure all criteria are met.

1.  During the decoking process, USA DeBusk receives real time data from numerous sensors

that transmit information to the on-board operator interface. Visual indicators on the interface
are represented as a spike when coke is encountered. The decoking technician has the ability
to stop the decoking pig and “scrub” an exact area to remove the coke from that region.

2.  The decoking technician constantly monitors the effluent returns to observe the amount of

“Black” or decoking fines that are entrained within the return effluent decoking stream. As the
pass becomes clean over time the visual “Black” in the stream goes to an unnoticeable
amount.

3.  Throughout the bi-directional pigging process used by USA DeBusk, the decoking pigs are

subjected to repeat encounters with the coke that is adhered to the internal walls of the tube /
pipe. This “back and forth” creates tracking and visual and physical markers on the decoking
pigs. The decoking technician monitors the condition of the pigs and makes adjustments to
size of the decoking pigs and the selection of decoking appendages to ensure 100% removal
of the coke.

4.  When the operators interface no longer indicates the presence of any fouling, the effluent
water contains no “Black” and the pig indicates no tracking or remaining discoloration, the
USA DeBusk shift leader will ask the client representative to come and observe first-hand the
final gauge foam run, The final foam will be line size or slightly over to ensure 100% contact
with the pass wall. The foam itself is constructed from a soft impressionable foam, to ensure it
marks, grooves or deforms if it encounters any remaining coke. The final foam is reviewed
jointly with the client and the client signs off on the cleanliness of the pass once the criteria is
met.

The stepped approached outlined above has proven very effective in the field and yielded successful
signs offs from all of our clients. The above system has also proven highly effective in gathering smart
pigging data on the first attempt.

Additional Services Available

HYDRO CUTTING
DSG crews have provided best-in-class hydro cutting services across the United
States and Caribbean for more than 20 years. We take pride in doing the job safely
and professionally – the first time and every time. With pressures up to 40,000 psi, our
hydro cutting services are ideal for tanks and vessels, demolition, FCCU turnarounds,
pipe cutting, door sheet openings, manways, concrete and metal.

PEP™ POLYMER EXTRACTION PROCESS
This highly effective chemical cleaning specialty removes polymer fouling in chemical
plant vessels and piping. Our proprietary PlastEx™ chemical cleaning agent is used
in the circulation process to dissolve polymer deposits uniformly, without dislodging
large pieces that can plug flow paths downstream. PlastEx™ is non-toxic, FDA-
compliant for food packaging, and classified by OSHA as non-hazardous and non-
carcinogenic. Material from the PEP process is recovered and can be separated into
liquid chemical and solid polymer components, with recovered chemical available for reuse.

CATALYST AND TRANSPORTATION

Transportation: USA DeBusk specializes in handling and transporting catalyst,

chemicals, waste by-products and raw materials. Products can be stored (bulk, super

sack, sea container, or tote), managed and distributed from our secured terminals. In

addition to transportation and storage, we offer trans loading services from vessels,

containers, railcars

Catalyst Shutdowns Services (Hot Catalyst Handling):

USA DeBusk provides hot catalyst handling and management. Our specialized

 vacuum  equipment  handles  catalyst  up  to  1,250°F  (677°C),  cutting  entire  days

from  your  shutdown  schedule.  Our  procedure  allows  removal  of  catalyst  during

unit shutdown, expediting operations withdrawal and turnover to maintenance. We

can also screen all refractory or support balls from the catalyst, then pneumatically

convey the material to storage for start-up or vessel for transportation – all in a 100% closed-loop system.
