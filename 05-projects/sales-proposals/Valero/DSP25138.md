Verification of Pass Cleanliness

USA DeBusk uses a combination of visual indicators, data review, and physical feedback to determine the
cleanliness of a decoking pass. This process is completed and reviewed side by side with the representative
of the client to ensure all criteria are met.

1.  During the decoking process, USA DeBusk receives real time data from numerous sensors that
transmit information to the on-board operator interface. Visual indicators on the interface are
represented as a spike when coke is encountered. The decoking technician has the ability to stop
the decoking pig and “scrub” an exact area to remove the coke from that region.

2.  The decoking technician constantly monitors the effluent returns to observe the amount of “Black”

or decoking fines that are entrained within the return effluent decoking stream. As the pass
becomes clean over time the visual “Black” in the stream goes to an unnoticeable amount.

3.  Throughout the bi-directional pigging process used by USA DeBusk, the decoking pigs are

subjected to repeat encounters with the coke that is adhered to the internal walls of the tube / pipe.
This “back and forth” creates tracking and visual and physical markers on the decoking pigs. The
decoking technician monitors the condition of the pigs and makes adjustments to size of the
decoking pigs and the selection of decoking appendages to ensure 100% removal of the coke.

4.  When the operators interface no longer indicates the presence of any fouling, the effluent water

contains no “Black” and the pig indicates no tracking or remaining discoloration, the USA DeBusk
shift leader will ask the client representative to come and observe first-hand the final gauge foam
run, the final foam will be line size or slightly over to ensure 100% contact with the pass wall. The
foam itself is constructed from a soft impressionable foam, to ensure it marks, grooves or deforms if
it encounters any remaining coke. The final foam is reviewed jointly with the client and the client
signs off on the cleanliness of the pass once the criteria is met.

The step approach outlined above has proven very effective in the field and yielded successful signs offs
from all of our clients. The above system has also proven highly effective in gathering smart pigging data on
the first attempt.

Time and Material Rate Charges

The following conditions will be billed at Time and Material (T&M) rates:

A. Project Delays

Delays beyond USA DeBusk LLC’s control, caused by the client, its employees, agents, or other

contractors, that extend any segment of the project beyond the scheduled timeframe, including but not

limited to:

•  Site Readiness Issues: Heater not prepared for work, no launcher connections as requested,

unavailable materials/equipment.

•  Operational Delays: Suspended work, untimely permit issuance (over 1 hour), logistical

conflicts with other contractors.

•  Safety & Environmental Factors: Plant safety alarms, site evacuations, unplanned

inspections, heater repairs.

•  External Conditions: Inclement weather, loss/insufficient power, inadequate utility supply.

•  Project Interruptions: Unplanned demobilization/remobilization.

Any work beyond the General Scope of Work will be subject to Time and Material charges.
